Motion
Motion is a prototype developed to prove the concept of using discrete units to control continuous surface. The kinetic sculpture showcases a piece of smooth, loose fabric suspended in the air by sixteen actuating steel cables placed at a 4x4 grid. By tinkering with amplitude, wavelength, and speed of wave motion, the kinetic sculpture displays drastic spatial dimensions through continuously running a sequence of unique compositions modeled on sea waves.

The seemingly still fabric is brought to life as one observes the seamless movement and tension of the fabric's physical form.
